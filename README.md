## Overview

Political violence is commonly tracked through two headline numbers: the *count* of recorded events and the *count* of fatalities those events produce. Using the six ACLED regional weekly aggregation files (March 2026 download), this project tests three claims on the common 2014–2025 window:

1. **Discrepancy exists.** Global annual events and fatalities follow statistically different trends.
2. **Event-type composition explains part of it.** A shift-share decomposition separates changes in the mix of `EVENT_TYPE` from within-type changes in per-event lethality.
3. **Regional robustness.** The global pattern is then probed inside each of the six ACLED regions.

A supplement extends the analysis to the full Africa series (1997–2025) where coverage is deepest.

## Data sources

- Six ACLED regional weekly aggregation files (`<Region>_aggregated_data_up_to_week_of-*.xlsx`) — primary source.
- ACLED codebook (`data/acled-codebook.html`) — consulted for category definitions.
- Coverage is uneven: Africa & Asia-Pacific begin 2014-01; Middle East 2014-12; Europe-Central-Asia and Latin America 2017-12; US-Canada only 2019-12. Cross-region analyses use the common 2014–2025 window; the coverage gap is flagged in the report.

## Methodology in one paragraph

All analysis is in Python. Weekly cells are concatenated into one panel (`01_data_cleaning.ipynb`), aggregated annually, and compared with Pearson/Spearman correlation and pooled log-linear regression with a series × year interaction to test whether the two trend slopes differ (`02_global_discrepancy.ipynb`). The shift-share decomposition of aggregate lethality $L_t = F_t / E_t = \sum_k s_{k,t} l_{k,t}$ into composition, within-type, and interaction components (`03_event_type_decomposition.ipynb`) is the standard Oaxaca-style identity. Both steps are then repeated per region (`04_regional_robustness.ipynb`) and on the long-run Africa series (`05_africa_long_run.ipynb`). The report (`docs/index.html`) is generated by `code/Z_generate_report.py`, embedding interactive Plotly figures saved under `docs/figures/`.

## Key findings

**RQ1 — Global discrepancy (2014–2025).** Events grew at **27.5 %/yr** in log-terms against fatalities at **12.3 %/yr**; the slope difference is statistically significant (pooled regression interaction, t = −3.06, p = 0.006). Per-event lethality fell from 1.91 to 0.59 (−69 %).

**RQ2 — Event-type decomposition.** Composition shift accounts for **27 %** of the lethality decline; **within-type** reductions account for **82 %**; interaction −10 %. Every event type showed falling per-event lethality, with Explosions/Remote violence seeing the sharpest drop (−78 %). Robustness check with `DISORDER_TYPE` gives the within-type share 92 %.

**RQ3 — Regional robustness.** The global pattern is real but heterogeneous. Africa and the Middle East drive the within-type narrative; Latin America is explained almost entirely by composition shift; Europe-Central-Asia is the exception (fatalities grew faster than events, driven by Ukraine from 2022); US-Canada enters too late to interpret.

**Supplement — Africa 1997–2025.** On the long series, events grew 12.0 %/yr and fatalities only 2.8 %/yr; lethality fell from 8.63 to 1.42. Splitting into pre-2014 and post-2014 reveals a structural break that the 2014 cut averages over.

## Limitations

- Uneven regional coverage in the cross-region window.
- ACLED sourcing has expanded over the period; a coverage-expansion story and a substantive-change story both predict the observed within-type lethality decline. The data alone cannot distinguish them.
- The shift-share decomposition is an algebraic identity, not a causal mechanism.
